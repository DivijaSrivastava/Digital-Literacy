Digital literacy is an individual's ability to find, evaluate, and communicate information using digital devices or digital media platforms. Digital literacy combines technical and 
cognitive abilities; it includes using information and communication technologies to create, evaluate, and share information, and critically examining their social and political impacts.
Digital literacy initially focused on digital skills and stand-alone computers, but the advent of the internet and social media use has shifted some of its focus to mobile devices.

Module 4: Email & Social Media Etiquette
This module focuses on the professional and interpersonal standards required for digital communication. It covers:
The Importance of Etiquette: Understanding why clear, respectful communication is vital in a professional setting.
Guidelines & Structures: Best practices for formatting emails, including proper subject lines, formal greetings, and professional signatures.
Workplace Standards: Navigating the nuances of digital interactions within a corporate or professional environment.

Module 5: Awareness of Cyber Crime & Financial Fraud
This module shifts toward digital safety and security, providing a comprehensive look at modern online threats:
Types of Cyber Crime: Identifying various forms of digital illegal activity at both personal and financial levels.
Financial Fraud: Specific focus on crimes within the financial domain and how scammers target accounts.
Prevention & Response: Practical measures for preventing cyber attacks and a flowchart to help learners understand how these crimes are executed and reported
